# Brand Guidelines: iThoddoo Maldives

## Brand Personality

iThoddoo Maldives should feel:
- premium but approachable
- local and trustworthy
- calm, sunny, and confident
- helpful without being pushy
- modern and mobile-first
- practical for booking decisions
- warm enough to feel human

The brand should communicate: "You are in good local hands."

## Luxury Design Principles

Luxury for iThoddoo Maldives is not loud decoration. It is clarity, confidence, space, and quality.

Principles:
- Use generous whitespace.
- Use strong photography.
- Keep layouts calm and readable.
- Let the destination be the visual hero.
- Avoid cluttered sections.
- Make booking actions clear.
- Prefer simple premium cards over heavy effects.
- Use high contrast for readability.
- Keep animations subtle if added.

Avoid:
- overly decorative gradients
- crowded hero sections
- too many colors on one page
- generic stock imagery
- low-quality or dark property photos
- unclear CTAs

## Color Palette

Primary:
- White: clean page base and premium space.
- Slate 950: dark footer, premium sections, strong contrast.
- Slate 900: headings and primary buttons.
- Slate 600/700: body copy and secondary labels.

Accent:
- Cyan 700: island, ocean, and category accents.
- Cyan 50: soft badge backgrounds.
- Green 500/600: WhatsApp and booking actions only.

Support:
- Slate 50/100: soft section backgrounds and cards.
- White overlays on hero imagery for labels and badges.

Usage rules:
- Use green for conversion, not decoration.
- Use cyan for brand accent and navigation cues.
- Use slate for structure and trust.
- Keep pages bright unless a dark CTA section is intentional.

## Typography

Voice through typography:
- bold, confident hero headings
- clean section titles
- readable body copy
- small uppercase eyebrows for structure

Recommended scale:
- Hero title: `text-5xl md:text-7xl`
- Section title: `text-4xl`
- Card title: `text-2xl` or `text-3xl`
- Body: `text-base` or `text-lg`
- Eyebrow: small, uppercase, bold, wide tracking

Rules:
- Avoid tiny body text.
- Keep line lengths comfortable.
- Do not overuse all caps.
- Keep hero copy short.

## Iconography

Icon style:
- simple
- recognizable
- functional
- supportive, not decorative

Use icons for:
- transfers
- rooms
- guest capacity
- amenities
- excursions
- location
- WhatsApp/contact

Rules:
- Keep icons consistent in size and tone.
- Do not mix too many icon styles in one section.
- Emoji can be used sparingly where the existing product tone supports it.
- For a more premium future UI, consider a single icon library such as Lucide.

## Photography Standards

Photography is central to trust.

Required qualities:
- bright natural light
- clear subject
- accurate color
- real places, rooms, food, beaches, boats, and experiences
- no heavy blur or dark filters
- no misleading luxury imagery

Property photography:
- exterior/front view
- room views
- bathroom
- breakfast or common area
- nearby beach or location context

Experience photography:
- actual activity
- visible water, boat, sandbank, reef, or guest context
- safe and realistic representation

Avoid:
- generic resort imagery
- unrelated island photos
- over-edited colors
- cropped images that hide important details
- low-resolution photos

## Writing Tone

Tone should be:
- clear
- local
- trustworthy
- concise
- helpful
- calm
- action-oriented

Good examples:
- "Send us your arrival time and we will suggest the best transfer."
- "Excursions are subject to weather and sea conditions."
- "Message us on WhatsApp for availability and pricing."

Avoid:
- exaggerated luxury claims
- promises about availability without confirmation
- vague phrases like "best ever" or "guaranteed perfect"
- long paragraphs on mobile pages

## CTA Style

Primary CTAs:
- "Book via WhatsApp"
- "Message on WhatsApp"
- "Ask About Transfers"
- "View Rooms"
- "Plan Your Trip"

CTA rules:
- Use green for WhatsApp booking.
- Use dark buttons for general navigation.
- Use white buttons on dark/image sections.
- Keep CTA text specific.
- Make the next step obvious.
- Avoid more than two primary CTAs in one hero.

## Mobile-First Design Principles

Most guests will browse on phones. Mobile quality is product quality.

Rules:
- Lead with the most useful action.
- Keep hero sections readable on small screens.
- Use one-column layouts first.
- Make cards easy to scan.
- Keep buttons large enough to tap.
- Avoid horizontal scrolling.
- Keep WhatsApp CTAs visible and clear.
- Test key flows on mobile before publishing.

Mobile page priority:
1. What is this?
2. Why should I trust it?
3. What can I book or do?
4. How do I contact?
5. What details do I need before booking?
