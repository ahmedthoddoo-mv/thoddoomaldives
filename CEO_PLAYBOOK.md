# CEO Playbook: Project Atlas

Project Atlas is the internal growth name for iThoddoo Maldives.

## Company Mission

Build the trusted digital platform for Maldives local island tourism, starting with Thoddoo and expanding island by island through local partnerships, structured inventory, and high-quality guest support.

## Quarterly Objectives

### Quarter 1: Foundation

- Finalize public website structure.
- Launch dynamic property platform.
- Add high-quality guesthouse and room images.
- Add at least 3-5 guesthouses.
- Improve excursion and transfer pages.
- Set up analytics for page views and WhatsApp clicks.
- Document operating processes.

### Quarter 2: Inventory and Conversion

- Add more guesthouse partners.
- Add structured excursions and local experiences.
- Create repeatable partner onboarding process.
- Improve SEO pages for stays, transfers, and excursions.
- Track leads manually or through a lightweight CRM.
- Improve mobile booking flow.

### Quarter 3: Platform Tools

- Plan Admin Dashboard data model.
- Build internal content management workflow.
- Standardize property, room, excursion, and transfer records.
- Prepare AI Concierge knowledge base.
- Improve partner reporting and lead tracking.

### Quarter 4: Scale Readiness

- Launch first version of Admin Dashboard or operator management tools.
- Test AI Concierge for guest questions.
- Prepare expansion playbook for the next island.
- Build investor materials and performance dashboard.
- Formalize commercial agreements with partners.

## Weekly Founder Checklist

- Review website traffic and top pages.
- Review WhatsApp inquiries and booking outcomes.
- Follow up with all open leads.
- Contact at least 3 current or potential partners.
- Add or improve one piece of destination content.
- Check transfer or excursion information for accuracy.
- Review mobile experience on key pages.
- Capture new photos or request better media from partners.
- Update task tracking and roadmap.
- Document one operational lesson from the week.

## Product Priorities

Highest priority:
- clear booking and inquiry paths
- accurate property and excursion data
- fast mobile pages
- high-quality images
- SEO-friendly destination content
- reusable platform architecture

Do later:
- complex payment flows
- advanced dashboards
- automated availability
- multi-language support
- AI Concierge

Product rule: do not build advanced systems before the manual process is understood.

## Guesthouse Acquisition Strategy

Target partners:
- guesthouses with reliable service
- properties near Bikini Beach or clear guest value
- owners willing to respond quickly on WhatsApp
- properties with or willing to provide good photos
- partners who understand commission or referral agreements

Acquisition steps:
1. Build a simple partner pitch.
2. Show example property page.
3. Explain benefits: visibility, direct leads, local platform trust.
4. Collect property details, room types, prices, amenities, photos, and WhatsApp number.
5. Add partner to the site.
6. Send preview link for confirmation.
7. Track inquiries and outcomes.
8. Improve listing based on performance.

Partner data checklist:
- property name
- slug
- description
- location
- distance to beach
- rating or trust signal
- WhatsApp number
- room names
- room prices
- room capacity
- room descriptions
- amenities
- hero image
- gallery images

Experience data checklist:

## SEO Strategy

Primary SEO clusters:
- Thoddoo guesthouses
- Thoddoo excursions
- Thoddoo airport transfer
- things to do in Thoddoo
- Thoddoo restaurants
- Thoddoo travel guide
- Thoddoo snorkeling
- Maldives local island stays

SEO rules:
- Create one strong page per search intent.
- Use clear titles and descriptions.
- Add real local details.
- Avoid thin generic pages.
- Keep pages fast and mobile-friendly.
- Use internal links between stay, transfer, excursion, and guide pages.
- Add images with meaningful context.

Measurement:
- search impressions
- organic visits
- WhatsApp clicks
- inquiry conversion rate
- top landing pages
- pages that need better content

- check WhatsApp inquiries

- collect first guest feedback
- improve unclear pages quickly

## Decision-Making Principles

Use these principles when choosing what to build or change:

- Guest trust comes first.
- Mobile experience is the default experience.
- Local accuracy beats generic scale.
- Simple working workflows beat complex unfinished systems.
- Reusable platform patterns beat one-off hardcoded pages.
- Every page should support discovery, trust, or booking.
- Do not publish uncertain prices, schedules, or availability as guaranteed.
- Build for Thoddoo first, but design patterns that can scale.
- Keep the brand premium, clear, and human.
- Measure conversion before adding complexity.
